# Understanding PPC Metrics: The Big Six

Amazon PPC (Pay-Per-Click) advertising is fundamentally a numbers game. Every decision you make — from which keywords to target, to how much to bid, to when to pause a campaign — should be driven by **data**, not gut feeling. The six metrics below are the foundation of every PPC strategy.

## 1. CPC — Cost Per Click

**Formula**: `CPC = Total Spend / Total Clicks`

CPC tells you how much you're paying every time someone clicks your ad. If you spent $100 and got 200 clicks, your CPC is $0.50.

**What it tells you**: How expensive your traffic is. Lower CPC means more clicks per dollar of budget.

**Healthy range**: Varies by category. Kitchen gadgets might see $0.50–$2.00 CPC, while supplements can exceed $3.00.

> **Key insight**: CPC is a **cost** metric — lower is generally better, but extremely low CPC on broad terms might indicate poor ad placement.

## 2. CTR — Click-Through Rate

**Formula**: `CTR = Clicks / Impressions`

CTR measures what percentage of people who see your ad actually click it. If 1,000 people see your ad and 50 click, your CTR is 5%.

**What it tells you**: How relevant and compelling your ad is to searchers. High CTR means your ad matches what shoppers are looking for.

**Healthy range**: Above 0.5% is acceptable, above 2% is good, above 5% is excellent for Amazon PPC.

> **Key insight**: Low CTR usually means your keyword targeting is too broad, or your ad isn't showing for the right searches.

## 3. CVR — Conversion Rate

**Formula**: `CVR = Orders / Clicks`

CVR tells you what percentage of ad clicks result in a purchase. If 100 people click your ad and 10 buy, your CVR is 10%.

**What it tells you**: How well your product page converts interested browsers into buyers. This is largely outside your ad control — it depends on your listing quality, price, reviews, and photos.

**Healthy range**: 8–15% is typical for Amazon. Below 5% suggests a listing problem.

> **Key insight**: You can have perfect ad targeting but if your listing doesn't convert, you'll waste money. Always optimize your listing alongside your ads.

## 4. ACoS — Advertising Cost of Sales

**Formula**: `ACoS = Ad Spend / Attributed Sales`

ACoS is the **most important efficiency metric** in Amazon PPC. It tells you what percentage of your ad revenue is being eaten by ad costs. If you spend $25 on ads and generate $100 in attributed sales, your ACoS is 25%.

**What it tells you**: How efficient your ad spend is. Lower ACoS means more profitable advertising.

**Healthy range**: Depends on your profit margin. Your ACoS should be below your profit margin to be profitable. For a product with 30% margin, target ACoS below 25%.

> **Key insight**: ACoS is the metric that directly connects ad spend to profitability. Every bid decision should be evaluated through the lens of: "Will this keep my ACoS below target?"

## 5. TACoS — Total Advertising Cost of Sales

**Formula**: `TACoS = Ad Spend / Total Sales`

While ACoS only considers attributed sales, TACoS compares ad spend to your **total** sales (including organic). This reveals whether your ads are truly driving incremental sales or just cannibalizing organic traffic.

**What it tells you**: The true cost of advertising relative to your entire business. If TACoS is rising while ACoS stays flat, your organic sales are declining — a warning sign.

**Healthy range**: Typically 5–15% for established products. Above 20% suggests over-reliance on ads.

> **Key insight**: A product with 20% ACoS and 5% TACoS is healthier than one with 15% ACoS and 25% TACoS. The second product depends entirely on ads for sales.

## 6. ROAS — Return on Ad Spend

**Formula**: `ROAS = Attributed Sales / Ad Spend`

ROAS is the inverse of ACoS. If ACoS is 25%, ROAS is 4x — meaning you earn $4 for every $1 spent on ads.

**What it tells you**: How many dollars of revenue you generate per dollar of ad spend. It's the "return" perspective, while ACoS is the "cost" perspective.

**Healthy range**: 4x or higher is typically profitable. Below 2x means you're spending more than half your revenue on ads.

## The Metrics Relationship

These six metrics are interconnected:

```
CTR → Clicks → CPC → Spend
                          ↓
CVR → Orders → Sales → ACoS → TACoS
                          ↓
                        ROAS
```

- **Better CTR** → More clicks per impression → Lower CPC (Amazon rewards relevant ads)
- **Better CVR** → More orders per click → Lower ACoS → Higher ROAS
- **Lower ACoS** → More efficient spend → Lower TACoS → Healthier business

## Quick Reference Table

| Metric | Formula | Good | Great | Direction |
|--------|---------|------|-------|-----------|
| CPC | Spend / Clicks | $1.00 | $0.50 | Lower is better |
| CTR | Clicks / Impressions | 2% | 5% | Higher is better |
| CVR | Orders / Clicks | 8% | 15% | Higher is better |
| ACoS | Spend / Attributed Sales | 25% | 15% | Lower is better |
| TACoS | Spend / Total Sales | 10% | 5% | Lower is better |
| ROAS | Sales / Spend | 4x | 7x | Higher is better |
