# Bid Elevator Prep: Ready to Bid

The Bid Elevator simulation tests your ability to make smart bidding decisions under realistic conditions. You'll face a sequence of bidding scenarios, each with different product characteristics, competitive landscapes, and strategic objectives. This lesson prepares you to think like a bidding professional.

## What the Bid Elevator Tests

The simulation evaluates your bidding across several dimensions:

**Accuracy:** How close are your bids to optimal? You don't need to hit an exact number — the scoring engine awards partial credit based on proximity to the reference bid. A bid that's 10% off scores higher than one that's 50% off.

**Reasoning consistency:** Do your bids reflect the scenario's constraints? If the scenario says margins are tight and the target ACoS is 15%, your bids should be lower than in a scenario with 40% margins and a 25% ACoS target.

**Strategic awareness:** Do you adjust bids based on competitive signals? If a scenario mentions aggressive competition on a keyword, you need to decide whether to compete or pivot.

## The Core Bidding Formula

Every bid you place should trace back to this calculation:

```
Max CPC = Price × CVR × Target ACoS
```

This tells you the **maximum** you can afford to pay per click while hitting your ACoS target.

**Example:**
- Product price: $29.99
- Expected CVR: 12%
- Target ACoS: 25%

Max CPC = $29.99 × 0.12 × 0.25 = **$0.90**

You should never bid above $0.90 for this product-keyword combination if your ACoS target is 25%. Any CPC above this pushes ACoS above target.

**But should you bid exactly $0.90?** Not necessarily. This is your ceiling. Your actual bid should factor in:
- **Competitive landscape:** If the market CPC is $0.50, there's no reason to bid $0.90 — you'll overpay.
- **Placement strategy:** If you want top-of-search, you may need to bid closer to your ceiling.
- **Confidence level:** New keywords with uncertain CVR deserve lower bids than proven performers.

## Reading the Scenario Brief

Each scenario in the Bid Elevator provides critical information. Read it carefully and extract these variables:

**Product economics:**
- Selling price
- Product margin (or cost, so you can calculate margin)
- Target ACoS

**Market data:**
- Estimated CPC range for the keyword
- Competition level (low, medium, high)
- Search volume indicator

**Strategic context:**
- Is this a launch (awareness priority) or established product (profit priority)?
- Is the keyword brand, product-specific, or category-level?
- Any seasonal or competitive dynamics mentioned?

## Bidding Decision Tree

Use this decision tree for each scenario:

**1. Calculate your max CPC** using the formula above. This is your hard ceiling.

**2. Check the market CPC range.** If the market CPC is above your max CPC, you have three options:
   - Accept a higher ACoS (not recommended unless strategically justified)
   - Target a less competitive keyword
   - Use Dynamic Down Only and set your bid at max CPC (you'll rarely win, but you won't overspend)

**3. If market CPC is below your max CPC**, set your bid based on intent level:
   - **High intent** (brand, exact product name): Bid near max CPC to win position
   - **Medium intent** (category terms, use-case terms): Bid at 60–80% of max CPC
   - **Low intent** (broad discovery): Bid at 30–50% of max CPC

**4. Adjust for strategy:**
   - **Launch mode:** Bid 20–30% above normal to accelerate visibility
   - **Profit mode:** Bid at or below the calculated max CPC
   - **Defense mode:** Bid aggressively on your own brand terms regardless of ACoS

## Common Bidding Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Bidding without calculating max CPC.** If you don't know your ceiling, you're guessing. Always do the math first.

**Mistake 2: Using the same bid for all scenarios.** A $30 product with 10% CVR and a $15 product with 20% CVR have very different max CPCs. Adjust per scenario.

**Mistake 3: Bidding above max CPC because "competition is high."** If you can't afford to win profitably, don't win. A sale at a loss is worse than no sale at all (unless you're in strategic launch mode and have a budget for unprofitable growth).

**Mistake 4: Ignoring the bid strategy context.** The scenario may specify which bid strategy is in use. Fixed bids = your bid IS your CPC ceiling. Dynamic Up and Down = your actual CPC could be up to 2x your bid (or 10x with placement adjustments). Factor this in.

**Mistake 5: Overbidding on broad terms.** Broad match keywords should always have the lowest bids in your account. They're research tools, not profit drivers.

## Practice Calculation

Try this before entering the simulation:

**Scenario:** You're selling a premium coffee grinder at $49.99. Your cost is $22.00. You're targeting 20% ACoS. The keyword "burr coffee grinder" has an estimated CVR of 8% and market CPC range of $1.20–$2.50.

**Step 1: Max CPC**
$49.99 × 0.08 × 0.20 = **$0.80**

Wait — the market CPC range ($1.20–$2.50) is above your max CPC ($0.80). You can't profitably bid on this keyword at 20% ACoS.

**What are your options?**
- Increase ACoS target: At 30% ACoS, max CPC = $1.20 (bottom of market range)
- Improve CVR: If listing optimization lifts CVR to 12%, max CPC = $1.20 at 20% ACoS
- Accept the keyword is too competitive and focus on less expensive terms

This is exactly the kind of decision the Bid Elevator tests — not just "what number do I type," but "should I even be bidding here?"

Launch the Bid Elevator simulation when you're ready. Each scenario will test a different bidding situation — approach each one fresh, do the math, and bid with purpose.
