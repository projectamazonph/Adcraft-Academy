# Campaign Architecture in Practice: Build Your First Campaign

You've learned about Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands, Sponsored Display, and campaign structure principles. Now it's time to put it all together. This lesson prepares you for the **Campaign Builder simulation**, where you'll design a complete campaign architecture for a realistic Amazon product.

## The Simulation Scenario

In the Campaign Builder, you'll work with a product from the **Kitchen Gadgets** category — a premium electric milk frother. You'll receive a mission brief with product details, target ACoS, and a keyword list. Your job is to build campaigns that achieve the business objectives.

## What You'll Be Evaluated On

The Campaign Builder scores you across five criteria, each weighted differently:

| Criteria | Weight | What It Measures |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Campaign Structure** | 30% | Are campaigns logically organized by match type and purpose? |
| **Keyword Selection** | 25% | Did you choose relevant, high-potential keywords for each campaign? |
| **Bid Reasonableness** | 20% | Are bids appropriate for each match type and keyword intent? |
| **Budget Allocation** | 15% | Is spend directed toward the highest-value campaigns? |
| **Negative Keywords** | 10% | Did you add relevant negatives to prevent wasted spend? |

The heaviest weight is on **campaign structure** — this is the foundation everything else builds on. A well-structured account with average keyword selection will outperform a poorly structured account with great keywords.

## Pre-Simulation Checklist

Before launching the Campaign Builder, make sure you can answer these questions:

**Campaign Structure:**
- Can you name the 3–5 campaigns you'd create for a single product?
- Can you explain why each campaign exists and what purpose it serves?
- Do you know which match types belong in which campaigns?

**Keyword Selection:**
- Can you distinguish between brand terms, product-specific terms, and category terms?
- Can you identify which keywords are high-intent (ready to buy) vs. research-intent (just browsing)?
- Do you know how to evaluate a keyword's potential based on relevance, estimated search volume, and competition?

**Bid Strategy:**
- Do you know the typical bid hierarchy (exact > phrase > broad)?
- Can you estimate a starting bid based on your target ACoS and expected conversion rate?
- Do you understand that bid = (CVR × Price × Target ACoS) gives you a break-even bid ceiling?

**Budget Allocation:**
- Can you allocate a total budget across campaigns using the 80/20 rule?
- Do you know the minimum viable budget per campaign (at least 10 clicks/day)?

**Negative Keywords:**
- Can you identify obvious negative keywords for a kitchen gadget product?
- Do you know when to use negative exact vs. negative phrase match?
- Can you spot cross-campaign overlap that needs negative keywords to prevent?

## The Building Process

When you enter the Campaign Builder, follow this sequence:

**Step 1: Read the mission brief carefully.** It contains critical information about the product, margin, target ACoS, and strategic priorities. Every decision you make should trace back to these constraints.

**Step 2: Plan your campaign structure first.** Before adding a single keyword, decide which campaigns you need and what purpose each serves. Write down the structure:
- Which campaigns will you create?
- What match type(s) in each?
- What's the budget for each?

**Step 3: Select keywords by campaign.** Start with your exact match campaign — choose the highest-confidence, most relevant terms. Then fill phrase match with category-level terms. Finally, add broad match discovery terms.

**Step 4: Set bids by match type.** Exact match gets the highest bids (you're confident these convert). Phrase match gets moderate bids. Broad match gets the lowest bids (you're paying to learn).

**Step 5: Add negative keywords last.** Review your keyword selections and look for overlap, irrelevant terms, and terms that don't match the product positioning. Add negatives to prevent waste.

**Step 6: Review your budget allocation.** Ensure your best campaigns get the most budget and that each campaign has enough budget to be viable.

## Common Pitfalls in the Simulation

**Pitfall 1: Creating too few campaigns.** Putting all keywords into one or two campaigns is the #1 mistake. It shows a lack of structural thinking and guarantees poor budget control.

**Pitfall 2: Setting the same bid for all keywords.** A brand term with 15% CVR deserves a much higher bid than a broad category term with 3% CVR. Adjust bids based on expected performance.

**Pitfall 3: Ignoring negative keywords entirely.** This is worth 10% of your score, but more importantly, it shows whether you understand the full picture of campaign management.

**Pitfall 4: Choosing keywords based on volume alone.** High-volume keywords that aren't relevant to the product will waste budget. "Coffee" might get millions of searches, but it's too broad for a milk frother.

**Pitfall 5: Not reading the mission brief.** The brief contains the target ACoS and product margin. If you don't know these, you can't set bids or budgets correctly.

## How the Scoring Engine Works

The Campaign Builder uses a deterministic evaluation engine that scores your submission against reference solutions. It's not looking for one "correct" answer — multiple campaign architectures can score well. What matters is:

1. **Internal consistency:** Your bids should reflect your keyword choices. High-confidence keywords should have higher bids than experimental ones.
2. **Structural logic:** Each campaign should have a clear purpose that's evident from its keywords and bid levels.
3. **Budget sense:** More budget should flow to campaigns with higher expected return.
4. **Negative relevance:** Negatives should prevent real waste, not just fill a list.

## Ready to Build?

You now have everything you need to design a strong campaign architecture in the simulation. Remember the core principles:

- **Structure first, keywords second** — your campaign organization is the foundation
- **Bid by confidence** — exact match gets the highest bids, broad the lowest
- **Budget by performance** — proven campaigns get the most spend
- **Negatives prevent waste** — don't pay for clicks that can't convert

Launch the Campaign Builder simulation when you're ready. Good luck!
