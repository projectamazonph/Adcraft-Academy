# Sponsored Brands & Display: Expanding Your Ad Strategy

While Sponsored Products drive the bulk of Amazon ad sales, Sponsored Brands and Sponsored Display serve critical complementary roles. Sponsored Brands build awareness and capture shoppers earlier in their journey. Sponsored Display retargets shoppers who showed interest but didn't buy. Together, they form the "surround sound" that amplifies your Sponsored Products performance.

## Sponsored Brands: Your Storefront Billboard

Sponsored Brands (formerly "Headline Search Ads") appear at the very top of search results — above even Sponsored Products. They feature your brand logo, a custom headline, and a selection of up to three products. Clicking the ad takes shoppers to your Amazon Store or a custom landing page.

**Key characteristics:**
- Available only to brand-registered sellers and vendors
- Requires an Amazon Store (or you can link to a product list page)
- You write a custom headline (up to 50 characters)
- Cost-per-click model, like Sponsored Products
- Appears on desktop, mobile, and tablet

The brand-registration requirement means not every seller can use Sponsored Brands. But if you own a brand, this ad type is essential — it's the only way to appear in the topmost position on Amazon search results with your own branding.

### Sponsored Brands Ad Formats

Amazon has expanded Sponsored Brands beyond the classic headline format:

**Product collection:** The original format. Shows your brand logo, headline, and three products. Best for driving traffic to specific products within your brand.

**Store spotlight:** Highlights your Amazon Store's sub-pages. Instead of showing products, it shows categories within your Store. Best for building brand awareness and encouraging browsing.

**Video:** A newer format that plays a video ad in search results. Extremely effective — video ads often see 2–3x higher CTR than static Sponsored Products because motion catches the eye. Best for products that benefit from demonstration.

### When to Use Sponsored Brands

Use Sponsored Brands when you want to:

**Build brand awareness.** Unlike Sponsored Products, which promote individual listings, Sponsored Brands promote your entire brand. The custom headline and logo create a branded experience that Sponsored Products can't match.

**Capture high-funnel traffic.** Target category-level terms like "kitchen gadgets" or "coffee makers" — terms that are too expensive and too broad for Sponsored Products but work well when you're showcasing a brand.

**Defend your brand terms.** Your own brand name is your most valuable keyword. Sponsored Brands at the top of search results for your brand name creates a "land grab" that competitors can't easily displace.

**Drive Store traffic.** If you've invested in building a beautiful Amazon Store, Sponsored Brands are the primary way to drive traffic to it.

### Budget Considerations

Sponsored Brands tend to have higher CPC than Sponsored Products because the top-of-page placement is premium real estate. However, they can also drive higher AOV (Average Order Value) because shoppers who click through to your Store often browse and add multiple items. Plan to allocate 10–20% of your total ad budget to Sponsored Brands, depending on your brand maturity.

## Sponsored Display: The Retargeting Engine

Sponsored Display is Amazon's programmatic display ad type. It lets you reach shoppers both on and off Amazon based on their shopping behavior. Unlike Sponsored Products and Brands, which only appear within Amazon's search results, Sponsored Display can follow shoppers across the web.

**Key characteristics:**
- Available to all sellers and vendors (no brand registration required)
- No keyword targeting — uses audience-based targeting instead
- Can appear on Amazon product pages, shopping results, and third-party websites
- CPC or vCPM (viewable cost per thousand impressions) bidding
- Quick setup — often just select a product and choose an audience

### Sponsored Display Targeting Options

**Contextual targeting:** Show your ad on product detail pages similar to yours, or in specific categories. This is "whoever is shopping for products like mine right now." Works like product targeting in Sponsored Products but extends beyond Amazon's ecosystem.

**Audience targeting:** Show your ad to specific groups of shoppers based on their past behavior:
- **Views remarketing:** People who viewed your product but didn't buy. This is the most popular use case — catching the "almost buyer."
- **Purchases remarketing:** People who bought your product (for re-purchase or cross-sell).
- **Complementary purchases:** People who bought products related to yours.
- **Substitute products:** People who bought products that compete with yours.
- **Lifestyle audiences:** Amazon segments shoppers into groups like "frequently buys fitness products" or "interested in home improvement."

### When to Use Sponsored Display

**Retargeting view-but-no-buy shoppers.** This is the highest-ROI use case. A shopper looked at your product, maybe read reviews, but left without buying. Sponsored Display can show your ad to them on other Amazon pages or even on external websites. Conversion rates for retargeting are typically 3–5x higher than cold targeting.

**Competitor conquesting.** Target shoppers who viewed or purchased competitor products. This is more expensive than organic conquesting but can be effective for new product launches.

**Off-Amazon reach.** If your customers browse Amazon but also visit other sites, Sponsored Display's extended network keeps your product visible.

### Budget Considerations

Sponsored Display is often the cheapest ad type on a CPC basis, with clicks frequently under $0.50. However, the conversion rate is also lower than Sponsored Products because you're targeting lower-intent shoppers. Allocate 5–10% of your budget initially, focusing on views remarketing for your best-converting products.

## The Ad Type Decision Framework

Here's a simple decision tree for choosing which ad type to use:

| Situation | Ad Type | Why |
|-----------|---------|-----|
| Promote a specific product for specific keywords | Sponsored Products | Most control, highest conversion intent |
| Build brand presence in search results | Sponsored Brands | Logo, headline, premium placement |
| Demonstrate how a product works | Sponsored Brands Video | Motion captures attention, explains value |
| Retarget shoppers who viewed but didn't buy | Sponsored Display | Highest ROI for remarketing |
| Target competitor customers | Sponsored Display + SP Product Targeting | SD for awareness, SP for direct comparison |
| Drive traffic to Amazon Store | Sponsored Brands | Only ad type that links to Store pages |
| Launch a new product with no sales history | Sponsored Brands + SD + SP Broad | Combine awareness (SB) + retargeting (SD) + discovery (SP) |

## The Full-Funnel Strategy

The most effective Amazon advertisers use all three ad types together in a coordinated strategy:

**Top of funnel (Awareness):** Sponsored Brands targeting broad category terms introduce shoppers to your brand. Sponsored Display lifestyle audiences reach new potential customers.

**Middle of funnel (Consideration):** Sponsored Products phrase and broad match capture shoppers who are actively searching within your category. Sponsored Display contextual targeting shows your product alongside competitors.

**Bottom of funnel (Conversion):** Sponsored Products exact match captures high-intent searches for specific terms. Sponsored Brands Video demonstrates product value for decisive shoppers. Sponsored Display retargets the almost-buyers.

**Post-purchase (Loyalty):** Sponsored Display purchase remarketing encourages reorders and cross-sells complementary products from your brand.

## Key Takeaways

- Sponsored Brands are your brand billboard — premium placement, custom headline, requires brand registration
- Sponsored Brands Video is a high-CTR format perfect for products that benefit from demonstration
- Sponsored Display is the retargeting engine — reach shoppers who viewed but didn't buy, both on and off Amazon
- Each ad type serves a different funnel stage; the best strategy uses all three in coordination
- Budget allocation: 70–80% SP / 10–20% SB / 5–10% SD is a solid starting point
