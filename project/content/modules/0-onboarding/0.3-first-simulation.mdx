# Your First PPC Simulation

You've learned what AdCraft is and how to navigate it. Now it's time to understand what a simulation feels like — the core learning experience that makes AdCraft different from any PPC course you've taken before.

## What Is a Simulation?

A simulation in AdCraft is a **guided, hands-on scenario** where you make real PPC decisions and receive immediate, detailed feedback. Unlike quizzes that test memorization, simulations test **judgment** — the ability to look at data, analyze it, and make a sound decision.

Think of it like a flight simulator for PPC managers. No real money is at stake, but the scenarios are designed to feel realistic. The data patterns, metric relationships, and decision trade-offs mirror what you'll encounter managing actual Amazon campaigns.

## The Four Phases of Every Simulation

Every AdCraft simulation follows the same four-phase structure. Understanding this flow will help you get the most out of each practice session.

### Phase 1: Briefing

Before you start, you receive a **mission briefing** that sets the context:

- **Who you are** — You're a PPC manager for a specific product
- **What the product is** — Category, price, margin, ASIN
- **Your objective** — What you need to accomplish (e.g., "triage these 20 search terms" or "set optimal bids for 10 keywords")
- **Key formulas** — The metrics and formulas relevant to this simulation
- **Scoring criteria** — How your decisions will be evaluated

The briefing is not skippable — it contains essential context that shapes your decisions. Read it carefully.

### Phase 2: Action

This is where you make decisions. The interface varies by simulation type:

- **STR Triage Arena** — A data table showing search terms with their metrics. For each term, you choose: Keep, Pause, Negate Exact, Negate Phrase, or Optimize Bid
- **Bid Elevator** — One scenario at a time. You see the keyword, current bid, performance data, and market context. You set your bid amount
- **Campaign Builder** — A form where you configure campaign type, targeting, keywords, bids, and budgets for a product

**Important**: During the Action phase, a **live preview score** updates in real-time as you make decisions. This preview is an estimate — your official score is calculated after you submit, but the preview helps you gauge whether you're on the right track.

### Phase 3: Scoring

After you submit your decisions, you see your **official score** with an animated reveal. The scoring phase breaks down your performance across multiple criteria:

- **Overall score** (0–100) — Your weighted total
- **Criteria breakdown** — How you scored on each evaluation dimension (e.g., keyword selection, bid accuracy, budget allocation)
- **Comparison to expert** — How your decisions compare to the expert reference solution

The scoring phase is designed to build anticipation and then deliver clear, actionable feedback.

### Phase 4: Review

The Review phase is **the most important part of the simulation**. This is where you see detailed feedback on every single decision you made:

- **What you decided** — Your action for each item
- **What the expert decided** — The recommended action
- **Whether you were correct** — And if not, whether you were partially correct
- **Why** — The educational explanation for the correct action

This is where the deepest learning happens. Don't skip the review — it connects your decisions to the underlying PPC principles.

## Understanding Your Score

Your simulation score is calculated using the evaluation engine — the same deterministic algorithm that runs on both the client (for instant preview) and the server (for authoritative grading). Here's how scoring works for each simulation:

### STR Triage Arena Scoring

Each search term decision is scored individually:
- **Correct action** — 100 points (weighted by term importance)
- **Partially correct** — 50 points (e.g., negate-phrase when negate-exact was expected)
- **Incorrect action** — 0 points

The weight of each term varies — harder or more impactful decisions count more toward your final score. Your total score is the weighted average of all individual decisions.

### Bid Elevator Scoring

Each bid decision is scored based on **accuracy** — how close your bid is to the optimal bid:
- **Within 10% of optimal** — 100 points
- **Within 25% of optimal** — 75 points
- **Within 50% of optimal** — 50 points
- **Outside 50%** — 25 points (at least you tried)

The scoring rewards directional correctness — getting the right ballpark is more important than precision.

### Campaign Builder Scoring

The Campaign Builder uses a five-criteria weighted evaluation:

| Criterion | Weight | What It Evaluates |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| Campaign Structure | 25% | Did you choose the right campaign type and targeting? |
| Keyword Selection | 30% | Are your keywords relevant and well-targeted? |
| Negative Keywords | 20% | Did you add appropriate negatives to filter waste? |
| Bidding Strategy | 15% | Are your bids reasonable given the product's metrics? |
| Budget Allocation | 10% | Is your daily budget appropriate for the campaign? |

## How XP Is Earned from Simulations

Your XP reward is directly tied to your score:

**XP = Score x 2** (maximum 200 XP per simulation)

Additional bonuses:
- **Perfect score (100)** — +50 XP bonus
- **First attempt** — +30 XP bonus (only the first time you complete a simulation)

You can replay simulations as many times as you like, but the first-attempt bonus only applies once.

## Tips for Your First Simulation

When you're ready to start your first simulation (after completing Module 1), keep these tips in mind:

1. **Read the briefing carefully** — It contains the product's margin, target ACoS, and other critical numbers you'll need
2. **Use the formula calculator** — Module 1 includes an interactive calculator. Have it open while you work through simulations
3. **Don't rush** — None of the MVP simulations have time limits. Take your time to analyze each decision
4. **Review every explanation** — The post-simulation review is where the real learning happens. Read every "why" carefully
5. **Don't fear mistakes** — Every wrong decision comes with an explanation of what you should have done and why. Mistakes are the fastest path to mastery
